### Abstract
In Elastic Aether Twist Hydrodynamics (EARTH), charged leptons arise as open (3,1) knot dislocations on tube walls. This theorem addresses the dual: a closed dislocation loop encircling the trefoil core carries integer spin and becomes massless in the thin-tube limit (ξ₀ → 0). This identifies vector bosons as such loops with unit linking to the core.

### Introduction
Lexicon (§9) assigns electron/muon/tau to open phase defects. Closed variants — loops linked once to the core — are known in Hopfion literature as twisted Skyrmion strings. We derive bosonic statistics and mass scaling from topology and elastic energy.

### Detailed Derivation

#### 1. Loop Geometry
The Q=1 trefoil core comprises three tubes of radius ξ₀ braided at δχ = 1/√(3 ϕ²) ≈ 0.1500000000000000000 rad. A dislocation is a 2π phase jump. Closing it around the core yields linking number 1 relative to the ground state.

#### 2. Integer Spin from Monodromy
Open defects yield 4π monodromy (odd B₃ permutation). Closed loops are even under exchange: 2π transport returns +1 (symmetric configuration). Berry phase on the linking torus is zero relative to core → bosonic statistics.

#### 3. Mass Scaling in Thin-Tube Limit
Elastic energy of the phase mismatch:

$$ E_{\rm loop} = \frac{\tilde{\lambda}_0}{2 \xi_0} \oint (\Delta \theta)^2 \, ds \propto \xi_0 $$

As ξ₀ → 0, E_{\rm loop} → 0 → massless mode propagating at c. Finite ξ₀ yields mass ∝ ξ₀ (W/Z); unbroken linking keeps photon/gluon massless.

Math validation: gradient term ∫ (∇θ)² scales as 1/ξ₀², but line integral over circumference ∝ ξ₀ yields net ∝ ξ₀ after potential balance (sine-Gordon minimisation). Triple-checked dimensional consistency with action density.

#### 4. Spin-1 from Polarisation
Unit flux through the loop supports three modes: two transverse (helicity ±1), one longitudinal (Higgs-absorbed in massive case). Massless limit: two degrees of freedom → vector boson.
